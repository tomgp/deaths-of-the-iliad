There—Pirithous’ son the rugged Polypetes skewered Damasus, pierced his bronze-sided helmet. None of the bronze plate could hold it, boring through the metal and skull the brazen spearpoint pounded, Damasus’ brains splattered all inside the casque— Polypoetes beat him down despite the Trojan’s rage, then Pylon and Ormenus, killed and stripped them both.

127, 128 & 129: Polypetes kills Damasus, Pylon and Ormenus

Homer, The Iliad book 12. Translated by Robert Fagles
