And tested veteran Leonteus speared Hippomachus, gouged Antimachus’ offspring down accross the belt, then drawing his long sharp sword from its sheath he rushed the front and took Antiphates first with a quick thrust, stabbing at close range— he slammed on his back, sprawled along the ground. Then Menon, Orestes, Iamenus—Leonteus killed the lot, crowding corpse on corpse on the earth that rears us all.

130, 131,132, 133 & 134: Leonteus kills Hippomachus, Antiphates, Menon, Iamenus and Orestes

Homer, The Iliad book 12. Translated by Robert Fagles
